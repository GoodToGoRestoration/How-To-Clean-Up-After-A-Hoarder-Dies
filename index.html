HOW TO CLEAN UP AFTER A HOARDER DIES

November 30, 2023

TABLE OF CONTENTS

Losing a loved one is undoubtedly one of life’s most emotionally challenging experiences. The overwhelming grief, the sudden void, and the realization that you won’t see that familiar face or hear that familiar voice again can be almost unbearable. In such moments, processing the depth of your loss becomes a journey of its own, one that takes time, patience, and support from those around you.
But what if, amidst this already heart-wrenching time, you find yourself facing another formidable challenge – a hoarded living space left behind by your departed loved one?
Hoarding disorder, a complex psychological condition characterized by the accumulation of excessive possessions, often leaves behind an overwhelming mess when a hoarder passes away.
Cleaning after a hoarder’s death is tough. It needs a careful plan and understanding of the problems involved. In this section, we’ll explore the key steps and considerations involved in the hoarding cleanup process.

Ensuring Health and Safety During Cleanup

Cleaning up after a hoarder’s death involves more than just tidying up clutter. It also requires being extremely cautious about health and safety. Hoarded spaces can harbor various hazards, both visible and invisible, that must be addressed to ensure the well-being of those involved in the cleanup.

Protective Gear

Before entering a hoarded space, it’s vital to wear appropriate protective gear. This includes:

  • Gloves: Protect your hands from potential contaminants and sharp objects.
  • Masks: Wear masks to safeguard against dust, allergens, and airborne particles.
  • Hazmat Suits: Depending on the condition of the space, hazmat suits may be necessary to shield the body from biohazardous materials.

Adequate Ventilation

Proper ventilation is crucial during hoarding cleanup. Open doors and windows to allow fresh air to circulate, helping to dissipate odors and improve air quality. Adequate ventilation also reduces the risk of inhaling harmful airborne particles.

Identify Biohazards

Hoarded spaces can contain biohazardous materials such as mold, animal waste, or spoiled food. Identifying and safely disposing of these materials is essential. If there’s uncertainty about handling biohazards, it’s best to seek professional assistance.

Safe Handling of Items

Exercise caution when handling hoarded items, especially if they have been stored in unsanitary conditions. Use proper techniques for lifting and moving objects to avoid physical injury.

Structural Safety

Assess the structural integrity of the hoarded space. Look for signs of weakened floors, walls, or ceilings. Structural repairs may be necessary to ensure safety.

Cleaning and Disinfection

Thorough cleaning and disinfection are essential steps in hoarding cleanup. Use appropriate cleaning products and methods to eliminate contaminants, allergens, and pathogens. Pay particular attention to high-touch surfaces.

Pest Control

Address any pest infestations that may have developed in the hoarded space. Pest control measures may be necessary to ensure the area is free from pests and their associated health risks.

Hoarding Cleanup Process – It is Good to Know!

Assessment and Planning

The first and crucial step in hoarding cleanup is the assessment of the hoarded space. This involves understanding the extent of the clutter, identifying potential hazards, and evaluating the overall condition of the property. A team of experts can help clean up hoarding by assessing the situation objectively and providing valuable assistance.
Once the assessment is complete, a detailed cleanup plan is developed. This plan outlines the scope of work, safety protocols, and a timeline for the cleanup process. It’s essential to approach hoarding cleanup with sensitivity, as the possessions often hold significant emotional value for the deceased and their loved ones.

Sorting and Categorizing

The next step involves sorting and categorizing the hoarded items. During this phase, items are typically divided into several categories:

  • Keep: Items of value, sentiment, or practical use that can be retained.
  • Donate: Items that can be safely donated to charitable organizations.
  • Dispose: Items that are no longer usable or safe and must be disposed of properly.
  • Hazardous Materials: Identification and safe removal of any hazardous materials or substances.

Professional hoarding cleanup teams use a systematic approach to ensure that valuable or sentimental items are preserved while eliminating potential health hazards.

Cleaning and Sanitizing

Once the sorting process is complete, the actual cleaning and sanitizing of the living space can begin. This phase involves removing clutter, debris, and any biohazardous materials. It’s essential to follow proper safety protocols, especially when dealing with potentially hazardous substances like mold, pests, or expired food items.
Specialized cleaning equipment and products may be necessary to restore the property to a safe and habitable condition. A thorough cleaning helps eliminate odors, allergens, and pathogens that may have accumulated over time.

Structural Repairs

In cases of severe hoarding, the accumulated clutter can lead to structural damage within the property. This damage may include weakened floors, walls, and ceilings. Structural repairs are essential to ensure the safety and integrity of the living space. Professionals may need to address these issues before the property can be fully restored.

Emotional Support

Throughout the hoarding cleanup process, it’s crucial to recognize the emotional toll it can take on family members and loved ones. Sorting through hoarders’ things after they pass away can bring up various feelings like sadness, regret, and fond memories. Seeking emotional support from friends, family, or mental health professionals is often necessary to cope with these feelings.
